Need more information?

Why did Nabo start?

Nabo was born out of the realisation that the sense of community we once experienced is struggling to exist in this technological age.

Nabo makes that same technology bring us together again.

How is Nabo different from other social networks?

Members verify their residential address to ensure that you're only talking to people from your neighbourhood.

Nabo is unique in that it is specifically designed to connect residents with each other and their suburb.

How is Nabo funded?

Nabo has secured funding from Reinventure Group (Westpac), Seven West Media and Internet entrepreneur and Nabo founder Adam Rigby.

Why should your organisation join Nabo?

Residents in your neighbourhood are already using Nabo to connect and discuss a range of local community issues including crime, safety, traffic and transport.

Members are looking for organisations to join the conversation.

How much does it cost for an organisation to join Nabo?

Nabo is free for members and organisations.

How long does it take to set up an account on Nabo?

Organisations can have their Nabo account established within 3-5 business days. Posting a message to Nabo is similar to other social networks and can take as little as 30 seconds.

How is Nabo moderated?

Any member of Nabo can report a message if they believe the message is inappropriate, abusive or commercial. Nabo organisations can moderate reported content on their page. Reported content will also be sent to Nabo Admin and our Suburb Leads.

Can businesses join Nabo?

At this time Nabo’s focus is on connecting the residents of a suburb with one another to create stronger and safer communities. We do not currently allow the promotion of businesses and services.

We do believe that there are some great synergies between Nabo, residents and local businesses and will be looking into these relationships in the future.
